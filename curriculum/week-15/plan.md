## Interview Prep Questions and Model Answers
### Q: Explain momentum in plain English.
A: Momentum should first be explained as an intuitive idea before any notation. In quant work it matters because it shapes how we measure, compare, or model financial behavior inside signals i: momentum, mean reversion, cross-sectional signals, and attribution tasks.

### Q: What is a common mistake when applying mean reversion in finance?
A: A common mistake is to use mean reversion mechanically while ignoring assumptions, data quality, or market frictions. In finance, even a mathematically correct method can fail if the data are noisy, the sample is unstable, or the implementation is unrealistic.

### Q: How would you test whether your work on signals i: momentum, mean reversion, cross-sectional signals, and attribution is robust?
A: I would start with a simple baseline, check sensitivity to assumptions, inspect subperiod stability, and verify that results survive realistic costs or constraints. If the topic involves prediction or backtesting, I would also guard against leakage and ensure evaluation is aligned with how the model would be used live.

### Q: Why does cross-sectional ranking matter for a quant trader or quant researcher?
A: Cross-sectional ranking matters because it changes either signal quality, risk control, implementation quality, or communication with a desk or portfolio manager. A useful quant answer should always connect the concept to a real decision rather than leaving it as theory only.
