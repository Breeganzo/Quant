# Personalized 24-Week Quant Learning Roadmap

## Assumptions
- Math is being rebuilt almost from zero, so each lesson starts with intuition and then adds technical language.
- Coding is good enough to move quickly once finance and math are tied back to practice.
- The 24-week plan runs from 2026-04-20 to 2026-10-04 if you start on the next Monday after this build.
- Weekly workload is sized for about 24 hours.

## 24-Week Roadmap Table
| Week | Dates | Theme | Key Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 2026-04-20 to 2026-04-26 | Reset Foundations: Python, returns, probability intuition, and market map | By Sunday you should be able to explain return, compounding, expected value, volatility, and buy-side versus sell-side in your own words without notes |
| 2 | 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-03 | Data and Linear Algebra: NumPy, pandas, visualization, diversification, and SQL basics | Can you clean a price table, compute returns, plot them, and explain why low correlation can reduce portfolio risk? |
| 3 | 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-10 | Math Rebuild I: Algebra, calculus intuition, probability rules, APIs, and Monte Carlo thinking | Can you explain derivative, probability, and Bayes in plain language and connect them to finance decisions? |
| 4 | 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-17 | Math Rebuild II: Statistics, inference, regression, optimization, and portfolio theory | Can you explain regression, overfitting risk, and the efficient frontier without equations first, then with equations? |
| 5 | 2026-05-18 to 2026-05-24 | ML Foundations I: supervised learning, regression, features, and train-test discipline | Can you design a small supervised learning problem in finance without leaking future information? |
| 6 | 2026-05-25 to 2026-05-31 | ML Foundations II: classification, metrics, imbalance, validation, and risk use cases | Can you explain when ROC-AUC is helpful and when it hides economically important errors? |
| 7 | 2026-06-01 to 2026-06-07 | Unsupervised Learning: clustering, PCA, latent structure, and factor intuition | Can you explain PCA as 'common movement directions' before using any matrix language? |
| 8 | 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-14 | Time Series I: stationarity, AR/MA/ARIMA intuition, walk-forward testing, and forecasting | Can you justify a walk-forward split and explain stationarity in trading language? |
| 9 | 2026-06-15 to 2026-06-21 | Finance Core I: market microstructure, execution, slippage, and transaction costs | Can you explain why a strategy that looks good in a notebook can fail in execution? |
| 10 | 2026-06-22 to 2026-06-28 | Finance Core II: portfolio theory, CAPM, factor investing, and optimization | Can you explain factor investing as a systematic bet on common drivers rather than individual stock stories? |
| 11 | 2026-06-29 to 2026-07-05 | Finance Core III: fixed income, yield curves, duration, convexity, and credit basics | Can you explain duration in words and use it as an approximation for bond price risk? |
| 12 | 2026-07-06 to 2026-07-12 | Finance Core IV: derivatives, options, Greeks, hedging, and risk management | Can you explain why options are nonlinear and why Greeks matter for risk control? |
| 13 | 2026-07-13 to 2026-07-19 | Quant Workflow I: idea generation, research logs, labels, and data hygiene | Can you explain one alpha idea with data source, target horizon, and failure mode? |
| 14 | 2026-07-20 to 2026-07-26 | Quant Workflow II: backtesting architecture, position sizing, and performance attribution | Can you name the most common reasons a backtest overstates real performance? |
| 15 | 2026-07-27 to 2026-08-02 | Signals I: momentum, mean reversion, cross-sectional signals, and attribution | Can you explain why the same raw return series may support one signal and reject another depending on horizon and costs? |
| 16 | 2026-08-03 to 2026-08-09 | Signals II: factor models, statistical arbitrage intuition, cointegration, and regime shifts | Can you explain factor-neutral thinking and spread intuition without overselling it as easy alpha? |
| 17 | 2026-08-10 to 2026-08-16 | ML for Quant I: trees, ensembles, nonlinear interactions, and model interpretation | Can you defend when not to use a more complex model? |
| 18 | 2026-08-17 to 2026-08-23 | ML for Quant II: volatility, EWMA, GARCH intuition, risk forecasting, and stress testing | Can you explain why risk models should be challenged with scenarios, not trusted mechanically? |
| 19 | 2026-08-24 to 2026-08-30 | Agentic AI for Quant I: research automation, literature review, idea generation, and guardrails | Can you explain why agentic AI is strongest in support roles and weakest when asked to invent trustworthy alpha without verification? |
| 20 | 2026-08-31 to 2026-09-06 | Agentic AI for Quant II: alternative data, NLP, experiment tracking, and robust research delivery | Can you prove that your process is reproducible and not just a one-off notebook? |
| 21 | 2026-09-07 to 2026-09-13 | Admissions Track I: target programs, scholarships, SOP structure, and recommendation strategy | Can you explain why each target school makes sense for your background and goals? |
| 22 | 2026-09-14 to 2026-09-20 | Interview Prep I: probability, statistics, mental math, Python, SQL, and markets discussion | Can you stay calm and structured when asked short technical questions aloud? |
| 23 | 2026-09-21 to 2026-09-27 | Interview Prep II: portfolio polish, GitHub presentation, networking, and mock case defense | Can a stranger understand your strongest project in under 3 minutes? |
| 24 | 2026-09-28 to 2026-10-04 | Final Integration: end-to-end capstone, final assessments, and application transition plan | Can you now show evidence, not just intention, that you are preparing for a serious quant master's path? |

## Benchmarking Against Top Programs and WorldQuant-Style Practice
- Berkeley MFE: interdisciplinary finance, statistics, computing, and industry project structure.
- Carnegie Mellon MSCF: financial computing, time series, machine learning, markets, and capstone orientation.
- Oxford Mathematical and Computational Finance: stochastic calculus, derivatives, risk, fixed income, deep learning, and dissertation-style depth.
- Columbia MSFE: optimization, stochastic models, foundations of financial engineering, deep learning, programming, and algorithmic trading orientation.
- WorldQuant University MScFE: practical sequence from data and econometrics through derivatives, stochastic modeling, ML in finance, portfolio management, risk, and capstone.

## Revision System
- Review weak items after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, and 14 days.
- Maintain an error log with: concept, specific mistake, corrected idea, next review date.
- Every Sunday: write a 10-minute summary from memory before looking at notes.
- Every capstone week: score yourself against the monthly rubric and list the next 3 corrections.

## Required Prerequisites
- Basic comfort with using a computer, terminal, and notebooks.
- Willingness to do arithmetic and algebra daily.
- Commitment to writing short explanations, not only code.
