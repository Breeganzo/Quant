### Q: What is a common mistake when applying validation and robustness in finance?
A: A common mistake is to use validation and robustness mechanically while ignoring assumptions, data quality, or market frictions. In finance, even a mathematically correct method can fail if the data are noisy, the sample is unstable, or the implementation is unrealistic.

### Q: How would you test whether your work on final integration: end-to-end capstone, final assessments, and application transition plan is robust?
A: I would start with a simple baseline, check sensitivity to assumptions, inspect subperiod stability, and verify that results survive realistic costs or constraints. If the topic involves prediction or backtesting, I would also guard against leakage and ensure evaluation is aligned with how the model would be used live.

### Q: Why does professional reporting matter for a quant trader or quant researcher?
A: Professional reporting matters because it changes either signal quality, risk control, implementation quality, or communication with a desk or portfolio manager. A useful quant answer should always connect the concept to a real decision rather than leaving it as theory only.


## Capstone
- Problem statement: Build one polished end-to-end quant research project with clear motivation, data handling, modeling, validation, risks, and business relevance.
- Data source suggestions: Use the cleanest public data source you have worked with during the plan, optionally combining market data with one engineered feature set or simple text signal.
- Implementation steps: Define the question, build the dataset, engineer features, establish baselines, test a strategy or forecast, include cost and risk checks, write a report, and prepare a presentation.
- Evaluation metrics: Research clarity, code quality, validation discipline, economic reasoning, communication quality, and honesty about limitations.
- Common mistakes: Trying to do too much, using too many models, hiding failure modes, or presenting a backtest without implementation assumptions.
