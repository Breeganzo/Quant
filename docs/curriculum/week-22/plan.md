## Interview Prep Questions and Model Answers
### Q: Explain mental math in plain English.
A: Mental math should first be explained as an intuitive idea before any notation. In quant work it matters because it shapes how we measure, compare, or model financial behavior inside interview prep i: probability, statistics, mental math, python, sql, and markets discussion tasks.

### Q: What is a common mistake when applying probabilistic reasoning in finance?
A: A common mistake is to use probabilistic reasoning mechanically while ignoring assumptions, data quality, or market frictions. In finance, even a mathematically correct method can fail if the data are noisy, the sample is unstable, or the implementation is unrealistic.

### Q: How would you test whether your work on interview prep i: probability, statistics, mental math, python, sql, and markets discussion is robust?
A: I would start with a simple baseline, check sensitivity to assumptions, inspect subperiod stability, and verify that results survive realistic costs or constraints. If the topic involves prediction or backtesting, I would also guard against leakage and ensure evaluation is aligned with how the model would be used live.

### Q: Why does coding clarity matter for a quant trader or quant researcher?
A: Coding clarity matters because it changes either signal quality, risk control, implementation quality, or communication with a desk or portfolio manager. A useful quant answer should always connect the concept to a real decision rather than leaving it as theory only.
